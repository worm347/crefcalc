Topic A: Coordinate Geometry

Lessons 1–5 · Distance, slope, and proving properties with coordinates.

01–03 — Lessons 1–3

Slope & Parallel/Perpendicular Lines

  • Slope formula: m = (y₂−y₁)/(x₂−x₁).
  • Parallel lines: equal slopes (m₁ = m₂).
  • Perpendicular lines: slopes are negative reciprocals (m₁ · m₂ = −1).
  • Horizontal line: slope = 0. Vertical line: undefined slope.
  • Use coordinates to prove that a quadrilateral is a special type.
Slope of AB = 2/3. A perpendicular line has slope −3/2.

04–05 — Lessons 4–5

Coordinate Proofs

  • Place figures strategically on the coordinate plane to simplify proofs.
  • Common placements: put one vertex at origin, one side along an axis.
  • Use distance formula to show sides are congruent.
  • Use slope formula to show sides are parallel or perpendicular.
  • Use midpoint formula to show diagonals bisect each other.
To prove a quadrilateral is a parallelogram: show both pairs of opposite sides have equal slopes.

Topic B: Area & Perimeter of Polygons

Lessons 6–10 · Calculating and comparing polygon measurements.

06–08 — Lessons 6–8

Area Formulas

  • Triangle: A = (1/2)bh.
  • Parallelogram: A = bh.
  • Trapezoid: A = (1/2)(b₁+b₂)h.
  • Regular polygon: A = (1/2)·apothem·perimeter.
  • On coordinate plane: use the Shoelace formula or decompose into triangles/rectangles.
  • All altitudes must be perpendicular to the base.
Trapezoid with b₁=6, b₂=10, h=4: A = (1/2)(16)(4) = 32 sq units.

09–10 — Lessons 9–10

Perimeter & Composite Figures

  • Perimeter: sum of all side lengths.
  • For composite figures: break into simpler shapes, find each area/perimeter.
  • When a region is 'removed,' subtract its area from the whole.
  • Coordinate perimeter: use the distance formula for each side.
  • Regular polygon perimeter: n × side length.
L-shaped figure: find total rectangle area, subtract the missing corner area.

Topic C: Interior & Exterior Angles

Lessons 11–15 · Angle sums in polygons.

11–15 — Lessons 11–15

Polygon Angle Sums

  • Interior angle sum of n-gon: S = (n−2)·180°.
  • Each interior angle of regular n-gon: (n−2)·180° / n.
  • Exterior angle sum: always 360° for any convex polygon.
  • Each exterior angle of regular n-gon: 360°/n.
  • Interior + exterior angle at each vertex = 180°.
Pentagon (n=5): interior sum = (3)(180°) = 540°. Each angle in regular pentagon = 108°.
