Topic A: Angles & Arcs in Circles

Lessons 1–5 · Central angles, inscribed angles, and arc relationships.

01–03 — Lessons 1–3

Central & Inscribed Angles

  • Central angle: vertex at center. Central angle = intercepted arc.
  • Inscribed angle: vertex on circle = half the intercepted arc.
  • Semicircle: 180°. Any inscribed angle in a semicircle = 90°.
  • Inscribed angles intercepting the same arc are equal.
  • Arc addition: adjacent arcs add up.
Central angle = 80° → arc = 80°. Inscribed angle intercepting that arc = 40°.

04–05 — Lessons 4–5

Angle Relationships in Circles

  • Interior angle (two chords): = (1/2)(arc₁ + arc₂).
  • Exterior angle (two secants, secant+tangent, two tangents): = (1/2)|arc₁ − arc₂|.
  • Tangent-chord angle: = (1/2)(intercepted arc).
  • A tangent is perpendicular to the radius at the point of tangency.
  • Two tangents from an external point are congruent.
Two chords intersect inside circle, intercepting arcs 80° and 120°: angle = (80+120)/2 = 100°.

Topic B: Segments in Circles

Lessons 6–9 · Chord lengths, secants, and tangent-secant relationships.

06–09 — Lessons 6–9

Chord, Secant & Tangent Segment Lengths

  • Two chords intersecting inside: a·b = c·d (products of segments are equal).
  • Two secants from external point: (whole₁)(external₁) = (whole₂)(external₂).
  • Secant + tangent from external point: (whole)(external) = tangent².
  • Perpendicular from center to chord: bisects the chord.
  • Congruent chords: equidistant from the center.
Chords: segment products 3·8 = 4·x → x = 6.

Topic C: Arc Length, Sector Area & Equations

Lessons 10–14 · Using central angles to find arc length and sector area.

10–12 — Lessons 10–12

Arc Length & Sector Area

  • Arc length: L = (θ/360°)·2πr = rθ (θ in radians).
  • Sector area: A = (θ/360°)·πr².
  • Think of arc length and sector area as 'fractions' of the full circle.
  • Full circle: circumference = 2πr; area = πr².
  • Segment area: sector area − triangle area.
r=10, central angle=90°: arc length = (90/360)·20π = 5π. Sector area = (1/4)π(100) = 25π.

13–14 — Lessons 13–14

Equation of a Circle

  • Standard form: (x − h)² + (y − k)² = r², center (h, k), radius r.
  • Rewrite by completing the square when given general form: x² + y² + Cx + Dy + E = 0.
  • Points inside the circle: (x−h)² + (y−k)² < r².
  • Points outside: (x−h)² + (y−k)² > r².
  • On circle: (x−h)² + (y−k)² = r².
Center (3, −2), radius 5: equation (x−3)² + (y+2)² = 25.
