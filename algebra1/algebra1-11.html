Two-Way Frequency Tables

Two-way tables display counts or frequencies for two categorical variables simultaneously.

  • Joint frequency: count in one specific cell.
  • Marginal frequency: row or column total.
  • Conditional frequency: proportion within a row or column.
  • Used to identify associations between categorical variables.
  • Relative frequency = count ÷ total.
In a survey: 30 students like math AND science, 50 total like math → P(science|math) = 30/50 = 0.6.

05–08 — Lessons 5–8

Probability Fundamentals

  • Theoretical probability: P(event) = favorable outcomes / total outcomes.
  • Experimental probability: from actual trials — may differ from theoretical.
  • P(A) is between 0 and 1. P(certain) = 1. P(impossible) = 0.
  • Complement rule: P(not A) = 1 − P(A).
  • Independent events: P(A and B) = P(A) × P(B).
  • Mutually exclusive: P(A or B) = P(A) + P(B).
Flip two coins. P(both heads) = (1/2)(1/2) = 1/4. P(at least one head) = 1 − P(no heads) = 3/4.

Expected Value & Simulation

Expected value uses probability to find the average outcome over many trials.

  • Expected value: E(X) = Σ [x · P(x)] — sum of each value times its probability.
  • Used in games, insurance, and decision-making.
  • Simulation: model a probability experiment using random numbers.
  • The law of large numbers: as trials increase, experimental → theoretical probability.
  • Fair game: expected value = 0 (or fair price for each player).
Roll a die: P(each face) = 1/6. E(X) = (1+2+3+4+5+6)/6 = 3.5
