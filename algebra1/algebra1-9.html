Topic A: Simplifying Radicals

Lessons 1–4 · Simplifying, adding, and multiplying radicals.

01–02 — Lessons 1–2

Simplifying Square Roots

  • √(ab) = √a · √b
  • Factor out perfect squares: √72 = √(36·2) = 6√2
  • Like radicals: same radicand. 3√5 + 2√5 = 5√5.
  • Multiplying: √a · √b = √(ab)
  • Rationalizing: eliminate radicals from denominators by multiplying by the conjugate.
√48 = √(16·3) = 4√3  ·  2√3 + 5√3 = 7√3  ·  √6 · √2 = √12 = 2√3

03–04 — Lessons 3–4

Rational Exponents

  • aˢ/ᵗ = (ᵗ√a)ˢ = ᵗ√(aˢ)
  • a^(1/2) = √a  ·  a^(1/3) = ∛a
  • Combine exponent rules with rational exponents.
  • Example: 8^(2/3) = (∛8)² = 2² = 4
27^(2/3) = (∛27)² = 3² = 9  ·  4^(3/2) = (√4)³ = 2³ = 8

Topic B: Solving Radical Equations

Lessons 5–7 · Isolating radicals and checking for extraneous solutions.

05–07 — Lessons 5–7

Solving Radical Equations

  • Isolate the radical on one side, then square (or cube, etc.) both sides.
  • Solve the resulting equation.
  • Check ALL solutions — squaring can introduce extraneous solutions.
  • Extraneous solution: satisfies the squared equation but not the original.
  • For √(expression): the result must be ≥ 0.
√(x+4) = 6 → square: x+4 = 36 → x = 32. Check: √36 = 6 ✓
