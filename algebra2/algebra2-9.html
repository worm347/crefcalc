01–03 — Lessons 1–3

Limits from Graphs

A limit describes the value a function approaches as x gets close to a point — not necessarily the value AT the point.

  • Notation: lim(x→c) f(x) = L means f(x) approaches L as x → c.
  • One-sided limits: lim(x→c⁻) from the left; lim(x→c⁺) from the right.
  • A limit exists only if the left-hand and right-hand limits are equal.
  • The limit can exist even if f(c) is undefined.
  • Limits at infinity: describes end behavior as x → ±∞.
f has a hole at x=2. lim(x→2) f(x) = 5, but f(2) is undefined — the limit still equals 5.

Topic B: Limits Algebraically

Lessons 4–6 · Direct substitution, factoring, and indeterminate forms.

04–06 — Lessons 4–6

Evaluating Limits Algebraically

  • Direct substitution: if f is continuous at c, then lim(x→c) f(x) = f(c).
  • Indeterminate form 0/0: factor, cancel, then substitute.
  • Rationalize: multiply by conjugate when radicals cause 0/0.
  • Squeeze theorem: if g(x) ≤ f(x) ≤ h(x) and both limits equal L, then lim f(x) = L.
lim(x→2) (x²−4)/(x−2) → substitute gives 0/0 → factor: (x+2)(x−2)/(x−2) = x+2 → lim = 4

Topic C: Continuity & Introduction to Derivatives

Lessons 7–9 · Continuity conditions and the concept of a derivative.

07–09 — Lessons 7–9

Continuity & Derivatives

A function is continuous at x = c if: (1) f(c) exists, (2) lim(x→c) f(x) exists, (3) they are equal.

  • Types of discontinuity: removable (hole), jump, infinite (asymptote).
  • Derivative: the instantaneous rate of change at a point.
  • f'(x) = lim(h→0) [f(x+h) − f(x)] / h — the limit of the difference quotient.
  • The derivative at a point = the slope of the tangent line to f at that point.
  • If f'(x) > 0, f is increasing; if f'(x) < 0, f is decreasing.
f(x) = x². f'(x) = lim(h→0) [(x+h)² − x²]/h = lim(h→0) (2x+h) = 2x.
